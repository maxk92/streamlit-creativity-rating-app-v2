# Creativity in Soccer

Creativity is defined as the product of originality and functionality. A creative solution must be **original** (*i.e., novel, rare, unusual*) as well as **functional** (*i.e., it must provide a solution to the given problem*).

Creativity in soccer describes the ability to choose solutions or execute actions in a given game situation that deviate from the norm, appear surprising, and are potentially advantageous for one's own team.

It arises from the conscious perception of the situation and the recognition of possibilities that others do not see, or see only later.

A creative action in soccer must be functional — that is, it must (potentially) bring one's own team closer to the game objective in the given situation (scoring goals, preventing conceding goals).

What matters here is the **intention or idea** and the **expected probability of success**, not the actual outcome of the action. This means that, for example, a pass with a plausible estimated chance of success can be considered creative even if it does not reach its target in the observed situation.
